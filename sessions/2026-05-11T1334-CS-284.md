## Decision pivot (the F.4 worked example)

The chat-side brief defaulted to dialect-suffix renames + /review dispatcher per cs237-diagnostic Priority 1. Pre-flight in CC plan mode (30 minutes of grounded reads) flipped the recommendation:

**Pre-flight finding:** `deploy.sh` stage 7 (lines 262-271) already copies each `per-repo/<dir>/agents/code-reviewer.md` into the corresponding product repo's `.claude/agents/code-reviewer.md`. All 4 product repos were confirmed populated. Claude Code's documented agent resolution rule (project-scoped > user-scoped at `subagent_type` invocation) already routes consumer-site calls to the correct per-dialect file by cwd. **The contract is operational today**; cs237-diagnostic D2's "global wins for skill listing" was about the cosmetic system-reminder enumeration surface, not runtime invocation.

**Implication for plan design:** Renames-plus-dispatcher would have changed ~30 LoC across 3 consumer sites (`/review` skill, `/implement` skill, `team-launcher` agent) + renamed 4 per-repo agent files. Native-precedence path requires zero file changes outside the new artifacts (ADR-018 + catalogue). The renames-plus-dispatcher design was rejected; the no-rename design was adopted.

**Pattern F.4 (cross-ticket assumption propagation):** Three weeks of accumulated framing — both chat-side (in successive adversarial-review passes) and CC (in plan-v1 write) — carried the cs237-diagnostic D2 premise as load-bearing fact. 30 minutes of pre-flight grounding (direct reads of `/review`, `deploy.sh`, the per-repo files, glaze) invalidated the premise chain. Mitigation rule (codified in the catalogue): verify-pre-flight-grounded-reads before inheriting any prior ticket's framing as fact.
